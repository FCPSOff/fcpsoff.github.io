FCPSOff Online

We wanted to inform you of a recent development regarding the use of Lightspeed Classroom
by our school district. While the intention of implementing this technology is to enhance the
learning experience, we have concerns about the potential negative impacts on students.
‎
Lightspeed Classroom has the ability to screen record and view students' recent search history,
among other features. While we understand that these capabilities are intended to
ensure a safe and productive learning environment, we are concerned about the
potential invasion of privacy and the impact on students' digital citizenship skills.
‎
We believe that it is important for students to learn how to navigate the digital world responsibly
and with an understanding of the potential consequences of their online actions.
The use of Lightspeed Classroom may undermine this learning process by providing a
"big brother"-like environment that may stifle students' natural curiosity and exploration.
‎
Furthermore, the use of screen recording raises concerns about the potential for misuse
or misinterpretation of students' actions. A student may be engaged in a perfectly innocent
activity, but if it is taken out of context, it could be misconstrued as something inappropriate.
‎
We urge the school district to reconsider the use of Lightspeed Classroom and to instead
explore alternative methods of promoting a safe and productive learning environment that
also respect students' privacy and digital citizenship skills.
‎
During this event, please avoid using our products during class periods in order to minimize
the risk of the discovery. Please keep programs closed, unless you are in a free period or at
lunch. Please be aware that this may very well be the end of this experiment.
‎
Thank you for your attention to this matter.
‎
- FCPSOff Development Team
